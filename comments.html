Aesthetics

As stated previously, I have decided to use a very simplistic approach for my overall aesthetics. I wanted to prioritise a 'minimalistic' creation that made it easy to navigate and was easy on the eyes. I have created a list below so summarise the aesthetics of my website:

  • Probably the most prominent feature of my entire website is the large title spanning across the middle of all my pages. I designed the font and format of this title in a way that it will grab the reader's initial attention when they load the website. They literally cannot miss it.

  • In terms of the colour palette, I have followed my minimalistic approach by opting to use only TWO colours consistently throughout my entire website; Orange and pale orange. However, using only two colours is never enough. In order for the elements on each page to look distinguished from eachother, I spaced them separately from eachother to allow the white background to peak through. This shows the clear segregation between the title, menu, heading and body of my website. As that famous saying goes, "Less is more".

  • When it comes to the font, after much deliberating (and trial and error) I decided to use verdana. Verdana is known as a 'sans-serif' type of font which are characterised by their modern and minimalistic look, reinforcing my vision to keep things simple. I set the font to be implemented throughout my entire website by using the '*' class in the css document.

  • Another part of the website I wanted to make sure kept a simple approach was the main div body on each page. By keeping the page background white and making the foreground of the text pale orange, I was able to design a more tapered style that helped strongly differentiate the foreground against the background.

Accessibility

Although not mentioned often, accessibility of the internet is truly one of the hallmarks that makes it great. Websites can remove physical barriers that may otherwise prevent people, especially with disabilities, from undertaking certain tasks or interactions.

However, on the contrary - if a website is badly designed with little to no accessibility, it can end up excluding those very same people from the web. Therefore I have compiled a list of all the accessibility measures I have implemented into my website below:

  • The first aspect of accessibility is providing alternative text to all my images. This was done by using the 'alt' attribute inside the 'img' attribute. Providing an 'alt' tag ensures that a user will be able to understand what the image is. For example, this is vital in the case of a slow connection, when images are switched off or when screen readers need to be used for visually impaired users.

  • Another way I have implemented accessibility into my website is by including the 'lang' (For language) attribute inside an 'html' tag for all of my web pages. This means that search engines and web browsers will have an easier time trying to locate and display my webpage.

  • The use of headings is also prolific throughout my website, especially in the content itself. This is important as it allows search engines to index the structure and content of my website. Another reason I have used headings is that users will always skim pages and find exactly what they need by looking at the headings. I have made simplicity of navigation one of my priorities. Additionally, headings have been made bold to differentiate content segments on my website.

  • Semantics is also one of the accessibility aspects of my website that I wanted to prioritise. Having HTML documents that are semantic means using the correct tags and elements to their respective purposes. For example, using the 'div' tag instead of the 'link' tag. Semantic HTML gives context to screen readers, which read the contents of a page out loud.

  • In regards to font, I have selected Verdana as it is easier to read for someone that may have reading difficulties. Verdana is a 'Sans-Serif' font, all of which are known to be simple and easy to read compared to 'Serif' fonts such as Times New Roman. Furthermore, I have also highlighted certain parts of content to be bold to emphasise their importance in a text. This makes my website easier to navigate when looking for a specific part. I have highlighted certain words throughout the website by using the 'b' tag.

  • I have also implemented a high colour contrast between the background and the actual content of my website. This helps to make the page much easier to read and cause less eye strain on the user.

  • Another important element of my website that promotes accessibility is the 'Go To Top' anchor at the end of each webpage. This allows users that scroll all the way to the end of the page to go back up in one click, instead of having to scroll back up.
